c:T6a3,<p>An employee must not take or take part in industrial action against an employer bound by collective agreement in order to support a demand in a matter that is regulated by the employer’s collective agreement:</p>
<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mt-4">
<li>if the industrial action has not been taken in the proper manner by the employee organisation;</li>
<li>if the objective of the industrial action is not to bind the employer and the employee organisation to a collective agreement;</li>
<li>if the employee organisation has not negotiated with the employer or the employer organisation on the demands made by the employee organisation; or</li>
<li>if the employee organisation demands that the employer apply a collective agreement that the organisation seeks to achieve in a way that supersedes the employer’s existing collective agreement.</li>
</ol> <p>Industrial action that contravenes the first paragraph is unlawful.</p>
<p>(Editor note: the unofficial government translation mistranslates this as "A collective agreement that contravenes the first paragraph is unlawful", and this has been corrected as part of the 666a data entry process)</p> <p>The general right to negotiation under Section 10 is applied in negotiations in accordance with the first paragraph even if the employee organisation lacks members who are employed by the employer. The obligation to negotiate does not apply if there have been impediments to the negotiations that are not attributable to the employer organisation.</p> <p>The provisions of this paragraph do not apply to industrial action aimed at supporting someone else’s lawful industrial action or a blockade over unpaid wages.
